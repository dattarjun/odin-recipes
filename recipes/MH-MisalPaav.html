Maharashrian Misal Paav

Description

Misal Pav is one of the most famous street foods from Maharashtra. It is a spicy curry made using sprouted moth beans, topped with farsan, onions, coriander, and served with soft pav bread. The dish is known for its rich flavor, spicy “tarri” gravy, and crunchy texture. Different regions of Maharashtra prepare Misal in different styles like Kolhapuri Misal, Puneri Misal, and Nashik Misal. It is commonly enjoyed as breakfast or evening snacks and is loved for its spicy and tangy taste. Misal Pav is not just food in Maharashtra — it is an emotion for many people. A hot plate of Misal with lemon and pav is enough to satisfy every spicy food lover.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups sprouted moth beans
  • 1 onion finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes chopped
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon goda masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Farsan mixture
  • Chopped coriander leaves
  • Lemon wedges
  • Pav bread

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds.
  2. Add chopped onions and cook until golden brown.
  3. Add tomatoes and cook until soft
  4. Add turmeric, chili powder, goda masala, and salt.
  5. Add sprouted beans and water, then cook for 15 minutes
  6. Prepare spicy tarri gravy separately if desired.
  7. Pour Misal into a serving bowl.
  8. Top with farsan, onions, and coriander.
  9. Serve hot with pav and lemon wedges.
